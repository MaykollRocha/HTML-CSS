Features

The PlayStation 2 (officially abbreviated as PS2) is a video game console produced by Sony Computer Entertainment (SCE). It was released on March 4, 2000 in Japan, on October 26 in North America and, later, on November 24 in Europe. It is the successor to the PlayStation. The PlayStation 2 is a sixth generation console, which competes with Sega's Dreamcast, Nintendo's GameCube and Microsoft's Xbox.

Due to its immense popularity around the world, the console, as well as its games, continued to be manufactured even after the launch of its successor, the PlayStation 3. Only 13 years after its launch, close to the announcement of the launch of the PlayStation 4 , that the Japanese newspaper Asahi Shimbun announced the end of manufacturing of the console in Japan on December 30, 2012. And on January 4, 2013, the British newspaper The Guardian announced that Sony had closed production of PlayStation 2 consoles in entire world.

The PS2 is the best-selling video game console of all time. According to data from Sony on March 31, 2012, more than 155 million PlayStation 2 units were sold[2] and more than 420 million units of exclusive console games. More than 4000 official games were released, licensed for the console.[5] The last game released for the PlayStation 2 was Pro Evolution Soccer 2014 released on November 8, 2013.
